Food guide

Welcome to a world of flavor! Where your pallete will surely be satisfied, find out what dishes could intrigue you!

Best for: Backpackers & Budget Travelers

Onigiri

Found at: 7-Eleven & FamilyMart convenience stores, nationwide Japan

Onigiri are hand-pressed rice balls wrapped in crispy nori seaweed, typically stuffed with fillings like pickled plum, tuna mayo, or salmon. A staple of Japanese convenience culture, they cost under ¥200 and can be found on every corner — making them the perfect quick, affordable bite for travelers on the go.

Best for: Food Enthusiasts & Foodies

Sashimi & Nigiri

Found at: Tsukiji Outer Market, 4 Chome-16-2 Tsukiji, Chuo City, Tokyo

Sashimi is thinly sliced raw fish served on its own, while nigiri pairs that same fresh fish over a small mound of seasoned rice. Both showcase Japan's deep respect for quality ingredients. The Tsukiji Outer Market in Tokyo is one of the best places in the world to experience both at their freshest, sourced directly from the morning fish auction.

Best for: Families

Tempura

Found at: Tempura Tsunahachi, 3 Chome-31-8 Shinjuku, Shinjuku City, Tokyo

Tempura is a classic Japanese dish of lightly battered and deep-fried seafood or vegetables, served with a delicate dipping sauce. Introduced to Japan by Portuguese missionaries in the 16th century, it has since become one of the country's most beloved comfort foods. Its mild, crispy flavor makes it a crowd-pleaser for all ages.

Best for: Solo Travelers & Late-Night Explorers

Ramen

Found at: Ichiran Ramen, 1 Chome-22-7 Nishishinjuku, Shinjuku City, Tokyo

Ramen is a deeply comforting noodle soup dish featuring wheat noodles served in a rich broth — ranging from light soy-based shoyu to thick and creamy tonkotsu. Each region of Japan has its own style. Ichiran is a famous solo-dining ramen chain where each seat is separated by dividers, letting you focus entirely on the bowl in front of you — a uniquely Japanese experience.
